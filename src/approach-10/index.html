Approach 10

Seed Derivation + Hue Channel

Only seeds are literal values: 3 base colors plus 2 per hue. Every other token (hover, soft, ink, separators, disabled, hierarchy) is derived with color-mix() in OKLab — a full dark mode is 3 redefined seeds. Components read a generic --hue-* channel published by data-color, with a neutral fallback: no role notion, any color works on any variant. Unlike existing approaches (all static hex tables remapped per theme×mode), the palette here is computed, and adding a theme or a hue means declaring seeds, not repainting every token.

Deliberately more opinionated than the badge: only design-system-vetted variants are exposed. primary, secondary and ghost still accept the optional data-color override through the --hue-* channel; danger and danger-soft are locked to the danger tokens. Every variant also exposes latent --ui-* hooks: undeclared by default, a theme or scope can define them to retarget a variant without touching component CSS (last strip).
